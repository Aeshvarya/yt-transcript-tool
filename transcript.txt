Hey everyone, welcome back to the channel. Today we're talking about why most people fail to stay consistent with their goals, and what actually works instead.

So the first thing to understand is that motivation is unreliable. Most people wait to feel motivated before they start, but motivation comes AFTER you start, not before. Action creates motivation, not the other way around.

The second point is about systems versus goals. A goal is "I want to read 50 books." A system is "I read 10 pages every morning." Goals are about the result you want. Systems are about the process that gets you there. People who focus only on goals burn out. People who build systems make progress automatically.

Third, and this is the big one: make it small. Everyone tries to do too much on day one. They go to the gym for two hours, get sore, and quit by day three. Instead, start with something so small you can't say no. Two pushups. One page. Five minutes. The point in the beginning is not progress, it's showing up consistently.

The last tip is to track it. What gets measured gets managed. Keep a simple streak or a checklist. Seeing the chain of done days makes you not want to break it.

So to wrap up: motivation follows action, build systems not just goals, start tiny, and track your consistency. Thanks for watching, and I'll see you in the next one.
